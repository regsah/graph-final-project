\section{Result Analysis}

The revised branch changes the project story in a meaningful way. Earlier
reports suggested that raw structural retrieval, especially uncorrected
Node2Vec, was the strongest direction. After the methodological corrections
introduced in the new branch, the highest-performing family is now the corrected
hybrid family:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{BGE pooled + Node2Vec (corrected)} gives the best Hit@10
    score, at 0.0867.
    \item \textbf{BGE-M3 + Node2Vec (corrected)} is essentially tied on Hit@10
    and slightly stronger on MRR, Recall, and MAP.
\end{itemize}

This result is more coherent with the actual objective of the project. Since
the system aims at article recommendation rather than pure graph completion, it
is reasonable that the best models combine semantic similarity with structural
context. The new branch therefore supports a more balanced conclusion: pure
graph structure remains useful, but semantic-structural fusion is now the most
convincing direction for the final system.

Another important result concerns pooling. In the previous concatenation setup,
BGE-M3 contributed 1024 dimensions while Node2Vec contributed only 128. The
new pooled models compress BGE-M3 to 128 dimensions before concatenation. This
does not produce a dramatic jump, but it does slightly improve the best top-line
Hit@10 result and makes the hybrid representation dimensionally balanced.

Earlier semantic-structural analysis also supports the project direction at a
more local level. Figure~\ref{fig:cosine-assortativity} shows that linked node
pairs tend to have higher cosine similarity than random node pairs. This result
does not by itself solve recommendation, but it verifies that textual-semantic
similarity carries a meaningful signal aligned with the graph structure.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.82\textwidth]{figures/cosine-similarity-assortativity.png}
\caption{Cosine similarity distributions for linked versus random node pairs. Linked pairs are shifted toward higher similarity, indicating that semantic closeness is informative for recommendation and evaluation.}
\label{fig:cosine-assortativity}
\end{figure}

Finally, the recommendation-oriented metrics reveal a second axis of model
quality beyond ranking accuracy. GCN Only remains weak on Hit@10 and MRR, but
it performs strongly on diversity-oriented metrics such as Aggregate Diversity,
Novelty, and Intra-List Distance. By contrast, BGE-M3 Only is more
popularity-driven. The corrected hybrid models occupy the most useful middle
ground: they retain competitive ranking quality while avoiding the strongest
popularity collapse.

\section{Verification and Consistency Checks}

The current results were not accepted at face value; they were repeatedly
checked and corrected during development. The main verification steps were:
\begin{itemize}
    \item validation of the revised phase-0 split statistics:
    216,123 undirected edges, 172,900 training edges, 43,223 test edges, and
    no new isolated nodes introduced by the split,
    \item re-estimation of the structural power-law coefficient on the revised
    branch, giving $\alpha \approx 2.9966$,
    \item separation of validation and test information during GCN and
    GraphSAGE training on the revised branch,
    \item consistency checking between notebooks, generated caches, and the
    final result tables included in the reports,
    \item correction of earlier notebook-state problems, including a malformed
    JSON notebook file and outdated cached results.
\end{itemize}

The GNN baselines were also verified independently through their link-prediction
test AUC values on the revised branch:

\centering
\caption{Revised-branch structural GNN verification results.}
\label{tab:gnn-auc}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
\textbf{Model} & \textbf{Validation AUC} & \textbf{Test AUC} \\
\midrule
GCN Only & --- & 0.9304 \\
GraphSAGE Only & --- & 0.9190 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

These AUC values confirm that both structural GNN baselines are learning
non-trivial graph structure, even if their recommendation ranking performance is
below the top hybrid models. Overall, the current branch can therefore be
considered both empirically informative and technically verified.
